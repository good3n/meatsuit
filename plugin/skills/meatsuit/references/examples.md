# Examples

Calibration pairs. Each shows a tell-laden draft and a humanized version, with the format
labeled so you can match register. These are for tuning your ear, not templates to copy.

The good versions aren't just *shorter* — they're more specific, less even, and they have a
point of view. Subtraction plus addition.

---

## Marketing / landing copy

**Before:**
> In today's fast-paced digital landscape, our cutting-edge platform empowers teams to unlock
> their full potential. By leveraging robust, AI-driven workflows, we deliver seamless
> collaboration that's not just efficient — it's transformative.

**After:**
> Our software handles the busywork your team hates: status updates, handoffs, the Friday report.
> Most teams save about a day a week. You set it up in an afternoon.

*Tells removed: "in today's fast-paced," "cutting-edge," "empowers," "unlock their full
potential," "leveraging," "robust," "seamless," em-dash reframe, "transformative." Added: a
concrete claim ("a day a week," "an afternoon").*

## LinkedIn post

**Before:**
> Excited to share a pivotal learning from this quarter. It's not about working harder — it's
> about working smarter. Three things made the difference: focus, clarity, and alignment.
> Grateful for a team that embraces change. 🚀

**After:**
> We killed our Monday standup this quarter and meetings dropped 30%. Nothing else changed.
> Output went up. Turns out the standup was where good plans went to get re-litigated.

*Tells removed: "pivotal," "not about X — it's about Y" reframe, forced rule-of-three,
"embraces change," emoji. Added: a real number and a real, slightly contrarian observation.*

## Blog intro

**Before:**
> In the ever-evolving world of software, one thing remains crucial: testing. Let's dive into
> why a robust testing strategy is the cornerstone of any successful engineering team.

**After:**
> We shipped a bug last month that cost us three days and one very patient customer. It would
> have been caught by a test that took four minutes to write. Here's the testing setup we use now.

*Tells removed: "ever-evolving," "crucial," "let's dive in," "robust," "cornerstone." Added: a
specific failure that earns the topic.*

## Email opener

**Before:**
> I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out to touch base regarding the
> aforementioned proposal and explore potential synergies moving forward.

**After:**
> Quick follow-up on the proposal. Two questions before we lock the budget — can we talk Thursday?

*Tells removed: "hope this finds you well," "touch base," "aforementioned," "synergies," "moving
forward."*

## Tweet / short post

**Before:**
> AI won't replace you. But a person using AI will. The future belongs to those who adapt.

**After:**
> I rewrote a script in 20 minutes that used to take me a morning. The skill now isn't writing
> the script. It's knowing it was wrong in two places.

*Tells removed: the "X won't replace you, but Y will" reframe, the empty aphorism. Added: a
concrete before/after and a real, specific point.*

## About / bio page

**Before:**
> A passionate, results-driven professional with a proven track record of leveraging innovative
> solutions to drive meaningful impact across dynamic, fast-paced environments.

**After:**
> I've spent eight years building payments systems, mostly the unglamorous part: making sure the
> money lands and the numbers reconcile. I've shipped at two startups and one bank.

*Tells removed: nearly every word ("passionate," "results-driven," "proven track record,"
"leveraging," "innovative," "drive meaningful impact," "dynamic," "fast-paced"). Added: tenure,
domain, specifics.*

## Internal comms / announcement

**Before:**
> Team, I'm thrilled to announce a transformative new initiative that will revolutionize how we
> approach collaboration and unlock unprecedented levels of productivity.

**After:**
> Starting Monday we're trying a new thing: no meetings before noon. Mornings are for focused
> work. We'll check in three weeks from now and keep it if it helps.

*Tells removed: "thrilled," "transformative," "revolutionize," "unlock unprecedented levels."
Added: what's actually happening, when, and how it'll be judged.*

## Edge case — a technical term that should stay

**Before:**
> Rails is a robust, powerful framework that empowers developers to leverage convention over
> configuration and seamlessly scale their applications.

**After:**
> Rails leans hard on convention over configuration, so a new app has sensible defaults out of
> the box. It scales fine until you're large enough to need something custom — and most apps
> never are.

*Note: "framework" stays — it's the correct technical word for Rails, not filler. "Robust,"
"powerful," "empowers," "leverage," "seamlessly" go. Don't strip a word just because it's on a
list; strip it when it's doing no work.*

---

## Edge case — preserve a formal register

**Before:**
> This agreement serves as a testament to the parties' mutual commitment and underscores the
> robust framework governing their ongoing collaboration.

**After:**
> This agreement records the parties' commitments and sets the terms governing their
> collaboration.

*Note: legal register is kept — it's still formal, not chatty. Only the inflation goes ("serves
as a testament," "underscores," "robust"). Humanizing isn't the same as casualizing.*

---

## Edge case — quoted AI text stays verbatim

When a draft *quotes* AI output to critique it, leave the quote alone and sharpen the writing
around it:

**Before:**
> The chatbot replied: "Certainly! I'd be delighted to delve into that topic." This kind of
> response, which is overly effusive and verbose in nature, tends to undermine user trust in a
> significant way.

**After:**
> The chatbot replied: "Certainly! I'd be delighted to delve into that topic." That tone — eager,
> padded, faintly insincere — is exactly what makes people stop trusting the thing.

*The quote keeps its tells (it's the evidence). The surrounding sentence gets fixed: "overly
effusive and verbose in nature," "in a significant way" out.*
